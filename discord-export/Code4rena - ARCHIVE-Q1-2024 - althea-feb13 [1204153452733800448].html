Althea is a protocol that enables flexible mesh networking through peer-to-peer payments, typically a WiFi router in a home is connected via antenna or fiber to another upstream router and that one to another until the network reaches the open internet. Christian | Althea 02/15/2024 16:48
Once Althea-L1 is deployed, real-world devices will have accounts on the chain. They will use these accounts to send and receive ERC20 tokens for a service, most likely because they are part of the Althea internet service protocol. Each device account can opt-in to becoming a Liquid account - they will have a LiquidInfrastructureNFT deployed and will automatically forward balances beyond a configured threshold to the NFT. Althea-L1 the chain will manage the flow of stablecoins from the device accounts to the LiquidInfrastructureNFT. If these LiquidInfrastructureNFTs are managed under a LiquidInfrastructureERC20, the LiquidInfrastructureERC20 will periodically withdraw stablecoin revenue from the NFTs and distribute the revenue proportionally to holders of the LiquidInfrastructureERC20. Liquid Infrastructure has not been deployed yet but will be shortly after the launch of the Althea-L1 chain.

Christian | Althea 02/18/2024 16:41
The Althea protocol enables peer-to-peer payments for internet service, and it will operate completely with a regular blockchain account. Althea does not need Liquid Infrastructure to run, these devices must send/receive tokens to participate in the network otherwise their internet access will be restricted. If someone is managing lots of Althea devices, they can tokenize their devices for easier management. With some new functionality that Althea-L1 will provide, the LiquidInfrastructureNFTs can be configured to send most of the tokens held by the device to a NFT contract (you want some working capital so that the device can always settle any debits it might create in its normal operation). If someone is deploying an entire Althea network and trying to handle complex ownership arrangements (e.g. you have several investors or maybe lots of smaller investors), distributing the revenue from the network is a large accounting burden. The LiquidInfrastructureERC20 can be used to make all of this simpler and pay out to all the involved parties in an automated fashion with little effort required by anyone. If you're the "owner" of the LiquidInfrastructureERC20 then you're managing the whole system. You may have legal responsibilities to ensure a fair payout between all the holders, the balance held by all the holders is very similar to shares. All LiquidInfrastructureNFTs involved with a LiquidInfrastructureERC20 must be owned by the ERC20. A user cannot "sell" the NFT because they do not hold it. If they unplug their device then they will no longer receive the service they're involved in.
