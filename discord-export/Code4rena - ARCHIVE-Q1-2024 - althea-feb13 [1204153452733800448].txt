[02/15/2024 16:29] christianalthea
There will be one Althea-L1 blockchain, and one LiquidInfrastructureNFT for any crypto-enabled real world asset that accrues revenue. These NFTs are not very liquid by themselves as they require manual management of their revenue flows so they can be bundled together under a LiquidInfrastructureERC20 as an asset class. The ERC20 manages revenue distribution and the owner of the ERC20 can mint new tokens and approve new holders as they please - this makes investment simple.

Initially we expect Althea to be the deployer and manager of just one LiquidInfrastructureERC20 with a few LiquidInfrastructureNFTs under management, however it will be open source so anyone could deploy the contracts for their own protocol as they see fit.


[02/15/2024 16:30] 0xjuancito
Is frontrunning possible in Althea?


[02/15/2024 16:39] christianalthea
Althea is a protocol that enables flexible mesh networking through peer-to-peer payments, typically a WiFi router in a home is connected via antenna or fiber to another upstream router and that one to another until the network reaches the open internet. The routers pay each other stablecoin ERC20s for the bandwidth they consume - usually between 5 cents and 25 cents. Beyond internet service, Althea makes it possible for devices to pay each other for anything.

Liquid Infrastructure makes it simple for people to invest in these networks by purchasing an ERC20, automatically accruing revenue over time as the tokenized assets funnel stablecoins through the LiquidInfrastructureNFT and from there to the LiquidInfrastructureERC20.


[02/15/2024 16:40] haxatron1

[02/15/2024 16:48] christianalthea
Once Althea-L1 is deployed, real-world devices will have accounts on the chain. They will use these accounts to send and receive ERC20 tokens for a service, most likely because they are part of the Althea internet service protocol. Each device account can opt-in to becoming a Liquid account - they will have a LiquidInfrastructureNFT deployed and will automatically forward balances beyond a configured threshold to the NFT. Althea-L1 the chain will manage the flow of stablecoins from the device accounts to the LiquidInfrastructureNFT.

If these LiquidInfrastructureNFTs are managed under a LiquidInfrastructureERC20, the LiquidInfrastructureERC20 will periodically withdraw stablecoin revenue from the NFTs and distribute the revenue proportionally to holders of the LiquidInfrastructureERC20.

Liquid Infrastructure has not been deployed yet but will be shortly after the launch of the Althea-L1 chain.

[02/18/2024 03:07] christianalthea
So there are multiple opt-in parts of the Liquid Infrastructure protocol which become more relevant when transactions happen on Althea-L1 with its extra CosmosSDK functionality. For the purposes of the Liquid Infrastructure protocol, the LiquidInfrastructureNFTs are meant to be sources of revenue and represent tokenized real world assets. On Althea-L1 the first devices like this will be tokenized router accounts involved in an Althea internet network, they will passively accrue revenue in stablecoins. If the owner of that device decides to tokenize it, then it will have a corresponding LiquidInfrastructureNFT which will eventually receive the revenue that device accrues.

From there it's possible to bundle multiple tokenized assets like this under a LiquidInfrastructureERC20 and have the revenue from each LiquidInfrastructureERC20 periodically distributed to holders.

The "AndDistribute()" part makes it easier to call, because burning is restricted if too much time has past and a distribution needs to happen.

[02/18/2024 16:41] christianalthea
The Althea protocol enables peer-to-peer payments for internet service, and it will operate completely with a regular blockchain account. Althea does not need Liquid Infrastructure to run, these devices must send/receive tokens to participate in the network otherwise their internet access will be restricted. If someone is managing lots of Althea devices, they can tokenize their devices for easier management. With some new functionality that Althea-L1 will provide, the LiquidInfrastructureNFTs can be configured to send most of the tokens held by the device to a NFT contract (you want some working capital so that the device can always settle any debits it might create in its normal operation).

If someone is deploying an entire Althea network and trying to handle complex ownership arrangements (e.g. you have several investors or maybe lots of smaller investors), distributing the revenue from the network is a large accounting burden. The LiquidInfrastructureERC20 can be used to make all of this simpler and pay out to all the involved parties in an automated fashion with little effort required by anyone.

If you're the "owner" of the LiquidInfrastructureERC20 then you're managing the whole system. You may have legal responsibilities to ensure a fair payout between all the holders, the balance held by all the holders is very similar to shares.

All LiquidInfrastructureNFTs involved with a LiquidInfrastructureERC20 *must* be owned by the ERC20. A user cannot "sell" the NFT because they do not hold it. If they unplug their device then they will no longer receive the service they're involved in.
